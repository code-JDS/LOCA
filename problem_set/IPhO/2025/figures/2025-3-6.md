### Fig. 6

The left panel of this figure shows a schematic diagram of the neck structure of a champagne bottle. The bottle mouth is sealed by a cylindrical cork of length $l_0$ and diameter $d$. The space between the bottom of the cork and the liquid surface is filled with carbon dioxide gas, with a pressure of $P_i$ and a volume of $V_G$. The volume of the liquid below is $V_L$, and the equilibrium between the gas and liquid phases satisfies Henry's law: $c_l = k_H P_i$. The atmospheric pressure is $P_0$.

The right panel of this figure illustrates the uncorking of two champagne bottles at different temperatures. In one scenario, a blue mist extends from the bottle mouth all the way down to the space above the liquid surface, and this mist rushes outward when the cork is removed. In the other scenario, a large amount of grey-white mist is generated and concentrated near the bottle mouth, but no grey-white mist is produced inside the neck cavity below.
