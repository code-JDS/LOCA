### Fig. 3

This diagram illustrates a schematic of the process of lifting a two-part barometer tube out of a liquid. The upper section of the two-part tube can be modeled as a cylinder with a cross-sectional area $S_b$ and a total length $H_b = 20\,\text{cm}$; the lower section (the open end) can be modeled as a cylinder with a cross-sectional area $S_t$ and a total length $H_t = 80\,\text{cm}$ ($S_b > S_t$). Here, we denote the height of the junction between the two sections as $h_t$ and the height of the liquid level inside the tube as $z_l$. (Initially, there is no air inside the tube, and the top of the tube is flush with the liquid surface; this implies that initially, $h_t = -H_b = -20\,\text{cm}$. When the bottom of the tube is just about to leave the liquid surface, $h_t = H_t = 80\,\text{cm}$.)
