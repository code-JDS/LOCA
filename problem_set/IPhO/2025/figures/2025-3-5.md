### Fig. 5

The left side of the figure shows a simple model of a Helmholtz resonator. It can be regarded as an empty bottle opening to the atmosphere through a neck of area $S$, with a piston of mass $m_p$ at the neck, where the atmospheric pressure is $P_0$ and the internal pressure is $P(t)$. A vertical coordinate axis $z$ is established along the neck to describe the piston's motion, with the positive direction pointing upwards. The origin $z=0$ is set as the reference position, and $z$ denotes the instantaneous vertical displacement of the piston.
The right side of the figure shows an air bubble (as an oscillator) of radius $a$. Upon bursting, the radius of the final circular opening at the liquid surface is $r$.
