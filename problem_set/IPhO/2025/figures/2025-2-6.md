### Fig. 6

This is a schematic diagram of a simplified version of the Cox's timepiece mechanism. The main structure of the apparatus consists of the two-part barometer tube and the mercury-filled reservoir previously mentioned in Part B; additionally, both the reservoir and the barometer tube are suspended by separate cables. Located above this main assembly is a horizontal surface with a mass block resting upon it. The left side of the mass block is horizontally connected, via an ideal pulley, to the cable suspending the reservoir, while the right side is similarly connected via a pulley to the cable suspending the barometer tube. Furthermore, two limiting devices are positioned on the left and right sides of the mass block to restrict its displacement to the range $-X < x < X$ (where $X > 0$).
