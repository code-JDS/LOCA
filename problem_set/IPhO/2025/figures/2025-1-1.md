### Fig. 1

This figure consists of two panels, (A) and (B).

Panel (A) presents a real visible-light image of a galaxy, providing the single piece of information that the galaxy's diameter is 18 kpc.

Panel (B) displays the rotation curve of the galaxy, illustrating the variation of rotation velocity $v_c$ [km/s] with respect to the distance $r$ [kpc] from the galactic center. The solid red line represents the theoretical velocity curve calculated based on a model where the galaxy's mass is concentrated within the bulge region ($r < 1$ kpc) and is uniformly distributed therein. It can be observed that the velocity follows an $r^{-1/2}$ decay outside the bulge (e.g., at $r = 10$ kpc, the rotation velocity is approximately 20 km/s). Furthermore, the figure includes observed data points, which reveal that the actual rotation velocity rises slowly beyond the bulge region and then essentially stabilizes at a level of approximately 160 km/s.
