Here is a detailed step-by-step solution to the problem.

### Analysis of the Forces and System Kinematics

The problem asks for the total tension force \(\vec{T}=T \overrightarrow{u_{x}}\) acting on the mass M when it is blocked at position \(x=X\) and the atmospheric pressure is the average pressure \(P_a = P_0\) (i.e., \(P_1=0\)).

The total tension force on the mass M is the vector sum of the forces from the two cables. Let \(T_R\) be the tension in the right cable (suspending the barometric tube) and \(T_L\) be the tension in the left cable (suspending the cistern). The total force is given by:
\[ \vec{T} = (T_R - T_L) \vec{u}_x \]
So we need to find the scalar component \(T = T_R - T_L\).

The tensions support the weight of the respective components:
1.  The right cable supports the barometric tube (empty mass \(m_{tb}\)) and the mass of the mercury inside it (\(m_{in}\)).
    \[ T_R = g(m_{tb} + m_{in}) \]
2.  The left cable supports the cistern (empty mass \(m_c\)) and the mass of the mercury inside it (\(m_{cis}\)).
    \[ T_L = g(m_c + m_{cis}) \]

The total mass of mercury in the system is constant: \(m_{total} = m_{in} + m_{cis} = \rho V_\ell\). We can express \(m_{cis}\) as \(m_{total} - m_{in}\).
Substituting this into the expression for \(T_L\):
\[ T_L = g(m_c + m_{total} - m_{in}) \]

Now, we can write the net tension force component \(T\) as:
\[ T = T_R - T_L = g(m_{tb} + m_{in}) - g(m_c + m_{total} - m_{in}) \]
\[ T = g(m_{tb} - m_c - m_{total}) + 2g m_{in} \]

We are given an initial condition: at \(t=0\), the mass M is at rest at \(x=0\), and the tensions are balanced (\(T_R=T_L\)), which means \(T=0\). Let \(m_{in}(0)\) be the mass of mercury in the tube at this initial state.
\[ 0 = g(m_{tb} - m_c - m_{total}) + 2g m_{in}(0) \]
This allows us to determine the constant term: \(g(m_{tb} - m_c - m_{total}) = -2g m_{in}(0)\).

Substituting this back into the general expression for \(T\) at any position \(x\) (while \(P_1=0\)):
\[ T(x) = -2g m_{in}(0) + 2g m_{in}(x) = 2g (m_{in}(x) - m_{in}(0)) \]
The net force on the mass M is directly proportional to the change in the mass of mercury inside the barometric tube. To find the force at \(x=X\), we need to find how \(m_{in}\) changes as the system moves from \(x=0\) to \(x=X\).

### Relating Mass Change to Displacement

A displacement \(x\) of the mass M causes the barometric tube to rise by \(x\) and the cistern to lower by \(x\). Let \(z_{tb}\) and \(z_c\) be the altitudes of the bottoms of the tube and cistern, respectively.
\[ z_{tb}(x) = z_{tb}(0) + x \quad \text{and} \quad z_c(x) = z_c(0) - x \]

The mercury levels inside the tube (\(z_{l,in}\)) and in the cistern (\(z_{l,cis}\)) are related by the barometric law. Since the pressure above the mercury in the tube is zero (\(P_{sat}=0\)) and the pressure on the mercury in the cistern is \(P_a=P_0\), we have:
\[ P_0 - 0 = \rho g (z_{l,in} - z_{l,cis}) \implies z_{l,in} - z_{l,cis} = \frac{P_0}{\rho g} = \text{constant} \]
Differentiating with respect to \(x\), we get \(\frac{dz_{l,in}}{dx} = \frac{dz_{l,cis}}{dx}\).

The total volume of mercury \(V_\ell\) is constant. We express it as the sum of the volume in the tube (\(V_{in}\)) and the volume in the cistern (\(V_{cis}\)).
\[ V_\ell = V_{in} + V_{cis} = \text{constant} \implies \frac{dV_{in}}{dx} + \frac{dV_{cis}}{dx} = 0 \]

The problem states that the mercury level \(z_{l,in}\) is always in the bulb. The volume in the tube is:
\[ V_{in} = S_t H_t + S_b (z_{l,in} - h_t) \]
where \(h_t = z_{tb} + H_t\) is the altitude of the tube-bulb junction.
The volume in the cistern is the volume of mercury up to level \(z_{l,cis}\) minus the volume of the submerged part of the tube:
\[ V_{cis} = S_c (z_{l,cis} - z_c) - S_t (z_{l,cis} - z_{tb}) \]

Now we differentiate these volumes with respect to \(x\), remembering that \(\frac{dz_{tb}}{dx}=1\) and \(\frac{dz_c}{dx}=-1\):
\[ \frac{dV_{in}}{dx} = S_b \left(\frac{dz_{l,in}}{dx} - \frac{dh_t}{dx}\right) = S_b \left(\frac{dz_{l,in}}{dx} - \frac{dz_{tb}}{dx}\right) = S_b \left(\frac{dz_{l,in}}{dx} - 1\right) \]
\[ \frac{dV_{cis}}{dx} = S_c \left(\frac{dz_{l,cis}}{dx} - \frac{dz_c}{dx}\right) - S_t \left(\frac{dz_{l,cis}}{dx} - \frac{dz_{tb}}{dx}\right) = S_c \left(\frac{dz_{l,cis}}{dx} + 1\right) - S_t \left(\frac{dz_{l,cis}}{dx} - 1\right) \]
Using \(\frac{dz_{l,in}}{dx} = \frac{dz_{l,cis}}{dx}\):
\[ \frac{dV_{cis}}{dx} = (S_c - S_t)\frac{dz_{l,in}}{dx} + S_c + S_t \]

Substituting these into the volume conservation derivative equation:
\[ S_b \left(\frac{dz_{l,in}}{dx} - 1\right) + (S_c - S_t)\frac{dz_{l,in}}{dx} + S_c + S_t = 0 \]
\[ (S_b + S_c - S_t)\frac{dz_{l,in}}{dx} - S_b + S_c + S_t = 0 \]
Solving for \(\frac{dz_{l,in}}{dx}\):
\[ \frac{dz_{l,in}}{dx} = \frac{S_b - S_c - S_t}{S_b + S_c - S_t} \]

Now we can find the rate of change of the volume of mercury in the tube:
\[ \frac{dV_{in}}{dx} = S_b \left(\frac{dz_{l,in}}{dx} - 1\right) = S_b \left( \frac{S_b - S_c - S_t}{S_b + S_c - S_t} - 1 \right) \]
\[ \frac{dV_{in}}{dx} = S_b \left( \frac{(S_b - S_c - S_t) - (S_b + S_c - S_t)}{S_b + S_c - S_t} \right) = S_b \left( \frac{-2S_c}{S_b + S_c - S_t} \right) \]
\[ \frac{dV_{in}}{dx} = -\frac{2 S_b S_c}{S_b + S_c - S_t} \]
Since this rate is constant, the change in volume \(\Delta V_{in}\) for a displacement from \(x=0\) to \(x=X\) is:
\[ \Delta V_{in} = V_{in}(X) - V_{in}(0) = \int_0^X \frac{dV_{in}}{dx} dx = \left(-\frac{2 S_b S_c}{S_b + S_c - S_t}\right) X \]
The corresponding change in mass is \(\Delta m_{in} = \rho \Delta V_{in}\).

### Final Calculation of the Force

Finally, we substitute this change in mass back into our expression for the force \(T\):
\[ T(X) = 2g \Delta m_{in} = 2g \rho \Delta V_{in} \]
\[ T(X) = 2g \rho \left( -\frac{2 S_b S_c}{S_b + S_c - S_t} X \right) \]
\[ T(X) = -\frac{4 \rho g S_b S_c}{S_b + S_c - S_t} X \]
This is the scalar component \(T\) of the total tension force \(\vec{T} = T \vec{u}_x\). The negative sign indicates that for a positive displacement \(X\), the net force from the cables on the mass M is directed to the left (in the \(-\vec{u}_x\) direction).

### Final Answer
The final answer is $\boxed{T = -\frac{4 \rho g S_b S_c}{S_b + S_c - S_t} X}$.
