This sub-problem asks for the relative error in calculating the maximal force \(F_{max}\) required to lift a tube filled with mercury, when the saturated vapor pressure of mercury \(P_{sat}\) is neglected.

### 1. Expression for the Maximal Force \(F_{max}\)

The problem states that for mercury, behavior B is observed. This means that as the tube is lifted, the pressure at the top of the mercury column drops to the saturated vapor pressure, \(P_{sat}\), before the tube is fully lifted out of the bath. The force required to hold the tube then reaches its maximum value, \(F_{max}\), and remains constant for any further lifting.

The equilibrium of vertical forces on the tube gives:
\(\vec{F} + \vec{F}_{gravity} + \vec{F}_{pressure} = \vec{0}\)

In terms of magnitudes along the vertical axis \(\vec{u}_z\):
\(F - mg - F_{pressure} = 0\)

The net pressure force is the sum of the force from the atmospheric pressure \(P_0\) acting downwards on the top surface of the tube and the force from the internal pressure \(P_{internal}\) at the top of the mercury column acting upwards on the inner top surface of the tube.
\(F_{pressure} = P_0 S - P_{internal} S\)

So, the equilibrium equation is:
\(F - mg - (P_0 S - P_{internal} S) = 0\)
\(F = mg + (P_0 - P_{internal})S\)

The maximal force \(F_{max}\) is reached when the internal pressure \(P_{internal}\) reaches its minimum possible value, which is the saturated vapor pressure \(P_{sat}\).
Therefore, the exact maximal force is:
\[F_{max} = mg + (P_0 - P_{sat})S\]

### 2. Approximate Maximal Force \(F_{max, approx}\)

The problem asks us to evaluate the error when \(P_{sat}\) is neglected compared to \(P_0\). This is equivalent to making the approximation \(P_{sat} \approx 0\).
Substituting \(P_{sat} = 0\) into the expression for \(F_{max}\), we get the approximate maximal force:
\[F_{max, approx} = mg + (P_0 - 0)S = mg + P_0 S\]

### 3. Expression for the Relative Error \(\varepsilon\)

The relative error \(\varepsilon\) is defined as the ratio of the absolute error to the exact value.
The absolute error is the difference between the approximate and exact values:
\[\text{Absolute Error} = |F_{max, approx} - F_{max}| = |(mg + P_0 S) - (mg + (P_0 - P_{sat})S)| = |P_{sat} S|\]
Since \(P_{sat}\) and \(S\) are positive, the absolute error is \(P_{sat} S\).

The relative error is then:
\[\varepsilon = \frac{\text{Absolute Error}}{F_{max}} = \frac{P_{sat} S}{mg + (P_0 - P_{sat})S}\]
This is the required expression for the relative error \(\varepsilon\).

### 4. Numerical Calculation

We are given the following numerical values:
*   Liquid: Mercury
*   Saturated vapor pressure: \(P_{sat} = 0.163 \text{ Pa}\)
*   Atmospheric pressure: \(P_0 = 1.000 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa}\)
*   Tube mass: \(m = 0.5 \text{ kg}\)
*   Tube cross-sectional area: \(S = 10 \text{ cm}^2 = 10 \times (10^{-2} \text{ m})^2 = 1.0 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}^2\)
*   Gravitational acceleration: \(g = 9.8 \text{ m} \cdot \text{s}^{-2}\)

First, we calculate the numerator of the expression for \(\varepsilon\):
\[P_{sat} S = (0.163 \text{ Pa}) \times (1.0 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}^2) = 1.63 \times 10^{-4} \text{ N}\]

Next, we calculate the denominator:
\[mg = (0.5 \text{ kg}) \times (9.8 \text{ m} \cdot \text{s}^{-2}) = 4.9 \text{ N}\]
\[(P_0 - P_{sat})S = (1.000 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa} - 0.163 \text{ Pa}) \times (1.0 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}^2)\]
\[(P_0 - P_{sat})S = (99999.837 \text{ Pa}) \times (1.0 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}^2) = 99.999837 \text{ N}\]
The denominator is:
\[mg + (P_0 - P_{sat})S = 4.9 \text{ N} + 99.999837 \text{ N} = 104.899837 \text{ N}\]

Finally, we compute the relative error \(\varepsilon\):
\[\varepsilon = \frac{1.63 \times 10^{-4} \text{ N}}{104.899837 \text{ N}} \approx 1.55386 \times 10^{-6}\]

Rounding to three significant figures (consistent with \(P_{sat}\)):
\[\varepsilon \approx 1.55 \times 10^{-6}\]

### Final Answer
The relative error \(\varepsilon\) is given by the expression:
\[\varepsilon = \frac{P_{sat} S}{mg + (P_0 - P_{sat})S}\]
Substituting the given numerical values:
\[\varepsilon = \frac{(0.163 \text{ Pa}) \times (1.0 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}^2)}{(0.5 \text{ kg}) \times (9.8 \text{ m/s}^2) + (1.000 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa} - 0.163 \text{ Pa}) \times (1.0 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}^2)}\]
\[\varepsilon = \frac{1.63 \times 10^{-4}}{4.9 + 99.999837} = \frac{1.63 \times 10^{-4}}{104.899837} \approx 1.55 \times 10^{-6}\]

\[ \boxed{\varepsilon = \frac{P_{sat} S}{mg + (P_0 - P_{sat})S} \approx 1.55 \times 10^{-6}} \]
