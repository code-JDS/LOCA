This problem requires us to determine the behavior of the force `F` needed to lift a tube out of a liquid bath for three different experimental conditions. The key physical phenomenon to consider is the formation of a vapor-filled space at the top of the tube when the pressure drops to the saturated vapor pressure `P_sat`.

### General Analysis

The force `F` required to hold the tube in equilibrium is found by balancing the forces acting on it: the applied force `F`, gravity `mg`, the atmospheric pressure force on the top outer surface `P_0 S`, and the force from the gas/liquid pressure `P_{internal}` on the top inner surface.

The force balance equation is:
`F - mg - P_0 S + P_{internal} S = 0`
`F = mg + (P_0 - P_{internal}) S`

Two regimes are possible:

**Regime 1: No vapor space**
As the tube is lifted, as long as the column of liquid remains in contact with the top of the tube, the height of the liquid column `z_l` is equal to the height of the top of the tube `h`. The pressure at the top of the liquid column is given by hydrostatics:
`P_{internal} = P_0 - \rho g h`
This regime is valid as long as `P_{internal} > P_{sat}`.
In this regime, the force is a linear function of `h`:
`F(h) = mg + (P_0 - (P_0 - \rho g h)) S = mg + \rho g h S`

**Regime 2: Vapor space exists**
If the tube is lifted high enough, the pressure at the top would drop to `P_{sat}`. At this point, the liquid vaporizes, and a space filled with vapor at pressure `P_{internal} = P_{sat}` forms. This transition occurs at a critical height `h = h^*` defined by:
`P_0 - \rho g h^* = P_{sat}`
`h^* = \frac{P_0 - P_{sat}}{\rho g}`

For any height `h \ge h^*`, the pressure inside is constant at `P_{sat}`. The force `F` also becomes constant:
`F = mg + (P_0 - P_{sat}) S`
This constant force is the maximum force, `F_{max}`.

### Behavior A vs. Behavior B

The observed behavior depends on comparing the critical height `h^*` with the total length of the tube `H = 1` m.

*   **Behavior A:** If `h^* > H`, the transition to Regime 2 never occurs within the experimental range `h \in [0, H]`. The force increases linearly for the entire process. The maximum force `F_{max}` is reached at `h = H`.
    `F_{max} = mg + \rho g H S`
*   **Behavior B:** If `h^* < H`, the system transitions from Regime 1 to Regime 2 at `h = h^*`. The force increases linearly up to `h^*` and then remains constant. The maximum force is the constant value achieved in Regime 2.
    `F_{max} = mg + (P_0 - P_{sat}) S`

### Calculations for Each Experiment

We use the given values:
`m = 0.5` kg
`g = 9.8` m·s⁻²
`S = 10` cm² `= 10^{-3}` m²
`H = 1` m
`P_0 = 1.000 \times 10^5` Pa

The weight of the tube is `mg = 0.5 \text{ kg} \times 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2 = 4.9 \text{ N}`.

**Experiment 1:**
*   `\rho = 1.00 \times 10^3` kg·m⁻³
*   `P_{sat} = 2.34 \times 10^3` Pa
*   Calculate `h^*`:
    `h^* = \frac{1.000 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa} - 2.34 \times 10^3 \text{ Pa}}{1.00 \times 10^3 \text{ kg/m}^3 \times 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2} = \frac{97660}{9800} \approx 9.97 \text{ m}`
*   Since `h^* = 9.97 \text{ m} > H = 1 \text{ m}`, the system exhibits **Behavior A**.
*   `h^*` is not applicable for this behavior.
*   Calculate `F_{max}`:
    `F_{max} = mg + \rho g H S = 4.9 \text{ N} + (1.00 \times 10^3 \times 9.8 \times 1 \times 10^{-3}) \text{ N} = 4.9 + 9.8 = 14.7 \text{ N}`

**Experiment 2:**
*   `\rho = 0.97 \times 10^3` kg·m⁻³
*   `P_{sat} = 47.4 \times 10^3` Pa
*   Calculate `h^*`:
    `h^* = \frac{1.000 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa} - 47.4 \times 10^3 \text{ Pa}}{0.97 \times 10^3 \text{ kg/m}^3 \times 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2} = \frac{52600}{9506} \approx 5.53 \text{ m}`
*   Since `h^* = 5.53 \text{ m} > H = 1 \text{ m}`, the system exhibits **Behavior A**.
*   `h^*` is not applicable for this behavior.
*   Calculate `F_{max}`:
    `F_{max} = mg + \rho g H S = 4.9 \text{ N} + (0.97 \times 10^3 \times 9.8 \times 1 \times 10^{-3}) \text{ N} = 4.9 + 9.506 \approx 14.4 \text{ N}`

**Experiment 3:**
*   `\rho = 0.96 \times 10^3` kg·m⁻³
*   `P_{sat} = 99.8 \times 10^3` Pa
*   Calculate `h^*`:
    `h^* = \frac{1.000 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa} - 99.8 \times 10^3 \text{ Pa}}{0.96 \times 10^3 \text{ kg/m}^3 \times 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2} = \frac{200}{9408} \approx 0.0213 \text{ m}`
*   Since `h^* = 0.0213 \text{ m} < H = 1 \text{ m}`, the system exhibits **Behavior B**.
*   The transition height is `h^* = 0.0213 \text{ m} = 2.13 \text{ cm}`.
*   Calculate `F_{max}`:
    `F_{max} = mg + (P_0 - P_{sat}) S = 4.9 \text{ N} + (1.000 \times 10^5 - 99.8 \times 10^3) \text{ Pa} \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}^2`
    `F_{max} = 4.9 \text{ N} + (200 \text{ Pa}) \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}^2 = 4.9 + 0.2 = 5.10 \text{ N}`
