### Part A.5: Fine and Hyperfine Structure

This problem asks for two main results:
1.  An expression for the fine structure energy difference \(\Delta E_F\) in terms of the fine-structure constant \(\alpha\) and the ground state energy \(E_1\).
2.  An expression for the wavelength \(\lambda_{HF}\) of a photon from the hyperfine transition, and its numerical value.

**1. Derivation of \(\Delta E_F\)**

The fine structure energy splitting \(\Delta E_F\) arises from the interaction of the electron's magnetic moment \(\vec{\mathscr{M}}_s\) with the magnetic field \(\vec{B}_1\) created by the proton's apparent motion. The interaction energy is given by \(E_{int} = -\vec{\mathscr{M}}_s \cdot \vec{B}_1\).

The problem defines \(\Delta E_F\) as the energy difference between the states where \(\vec{\mathscr{M}}_s\) is anti-parallel and parallel to \(\vec{B}_1\).
-   Energy when parallel: \(E_{\parallel} = -\mathscr{M}_s B_1\)
-   Energy when anti-parallel: \(E_{\nparallel} = +\mathscr{M}_s B_1\)

The energy difference is therefore:
\[\Delta E_F = E_{\nparallel} - E_{\parallel} = (\mathscr{M}_s B_1) - (-\mathscr{M}_s B_1) = 2 \mathscr{M}_s B_1\]

From the problem statement, we are given the magnitude of the electron's magnetic moment as \(\mathscr{M}_s = \frac{e\hbar}{m_e}\).
From the solution to sub-problem A.4, the magnitude of the magnetic field is \(B_1 = \frac{\mu_0 e \alpha c}{4\pi r_1^2}\).

Substituting these into the expression for \(\Delta E_F\):
\[\Delta E_F = 2 \left(\frac{e\hbar}{m_e}\right) \left(\frac{\mu_0 e \alpha c}{4\pi r_1^2}\right) = \frac{\mu_0 e^2 \hbar \alpha c}{2\pi m_e r_1^2}\]

To express this in terms of \(\alpha\) and \(E_1\), we use the following relations:
-   From electromagnetism: \(\mu_0 \varepsilon_0 = 1/c^2 \implies \mu_0 = 1/(\varepsilon_0 c^2)\)
-   Definition of \(\alpha\): \(\alpha = \frac{e^2}{4\pi\varepsilon_0 \hbar c} \implies \frac{e^2}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} = \alpha \hbar c\)
-   From sub-problem A.2: \(r_1 = \frac{\hbar}{m_e c \alpha}\)
-   From sub-problem A.3: \(E_1 = -\frac{1}{2} m_e c^2 \alpha^2 \implies |E_1| = \frac{1}{2} m_e c^2 \alpha^2\)

Let's simplify the expression for \(\Delta E_F\):
\[\Delta E_F = \frac{1}{\varepsilon_0 c^2} \frac{e^2 \hbar \alpha c}{2\pi m_e r_1^2} = \frac{e^2 \hbar \alpha}{2\pi \varepsilon_0 m_e c r_1^2}\]
Substitute the expression for \(r_1\):
\[\Delta E_F = \frac{e^2 \hbar \alpha}{2\pi \varepsilon_0 m_e c} \left(\frac{m_e c \alpha}{\hbar}\right)^2 = \frac{e^2 m_e c \alpha^3}{2\pi \varepsilon_0 \hbar}\]
Now, we can use the definition of \(\alpha\) to simplify further:
\[\Delta E_F = 2 \left(\frac{e^2}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\right) \frac{m_e c \alpha^3}{\hbar} = 2 (\alpha \hbar c) \frac{m_e c \alpha^3}{\hbar} = 2 m_e c^2 \alpha^4\]
Finally, we express this in terms of \(E_1\). Since \(|E_1| = \frac{1}{2} m_e c^2 \alpha^2\), we have \(m_e c^2 = \frac{2|E_1|}{\alpha^2}\).
\[\Delta E_F = 2 \left(\frac{2|E_1|}{\alpha^2}\right) \alpha^4 = 4\alpha^2 |E_1|\]
Since \(E_1\) is a negative quantity, we can write this as:
\[\Delta E_F = -4\alpha^2 E_1\]

**2. Derivation of \(\lambda_{HF}\)**

The energy of a photon emitted during the hyperfine transition is \(\Delta E_{HF}\). The problem provides the relation:
\[\Delta E_{HF} \simeq 3.72 \frac{m_e}{m_p} \Delta E_F\]
The wavelength \(\lambda_{HF}\) of this photon is related to its energy by \(\Delta E_{HF} = \frac{hc}{\lambda_{HF}} = \frac{2\pi\hbar c}{\lambda_{HF}}\).
Therefore, the expression for the wavelength is:
\[\lambda_{HF} = \frac{2\pi\hbar c}{\Delta E_{HF}} = \frac{2\pi\hbar c}{3.72 \frac{m_e}{m_p} \Delta E_F}\]
Substituting the expression for \(\Delta E_F = 4\alpha^2 |E_1|\):
\[\lambda_{HF} = \frac{2\pi\hbar c}{3.72 \frac{m_e}{m_p} (4\alpha^2 |E_1|)} = \frac{\pi\hbar c}{7.44 \frac{m_e}{m_p} \alpha^2 |E_1|}\]

To calculate the numerical value, we first find \(\Delta E_{HF}\) in electron-volts (eV).
Using the values from previous sub-problems and the problem statement:
-   \(\alpha \approx 7.27 \times 10^{-3}\)
-   \(|E_1| \approx 13.5 \text{ eV}\)
-   \(m_e = 9.109 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}\)
-   \(m_p = 1.672 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}\)
The ratio of masses is \(\frac{m_e}{m_p} \approx \frac{9.109 \times 10^{-31}}{1.672 \times 10^{-27}} \approx 5.448 \times 10^{-4}\).

First, calculate \(\Delta E_F\):
\[\Delta E_F = 4\alpha^2 |E_1| = 4 \times (7.27 \times 10^{-3})^2 \times 13.5 \text{ eV} \approx 2.85 \times 10^{-3} \text{ eV}\]
Now, calculate \(\Delta E_{HF}\):
\[\Delta E_{HF} \approx 3.72 \times (5.448 \times 10^{-4}) \times (2.85 \times 10^{-3} \text{ eV}) \approx 5.77 \times 10^{-6} \text{ eV}\]
Finally, we calculate the wavelength using \(hc \approx 1240 \text{ eV}\cdot\text{nm}\):
\[\lambda_{HF} = \frac{hc}{\Delta E_{HF}} \approx \frac{1240 \text{ eV}\cdot\text{nm}}{5.77 \times 10^{-6} \text{ eV}} \approx 2.15 \times 10^8 \text{ nm}\]
Converting this to meters and centimeters:
\[\lambda_{HF} \approx 0.215 \text{ m} = 21.5 \text{ cm}\]
Rounding to two significant digits as requested:
\[\lambda_{HF} \approx 21 \text{ cm}\]

### Final Answer
The fine structure energy difference is expressed as a function of \(\alpha\) and \(E_1\) as:
\[ \Delta E_F = -4\alpha^2 E_1 \]
The wavelength of a photon emitted during a transition between the two states of the hyperfine structure is given by the expression:
\[ \lambda_{HF} = \frac{2\pi\hbar c}{3.72 \frac{m_e}{m_p} \Delta E_F} = \frac{\pi\hbar c}{-7.44 \frac{m_e}{m_p} \alpha^2 E_1} \]
Its numerical value is:
\[ \boxed{\lambda_{HF} \approx 21 \text{ cm}} \]
