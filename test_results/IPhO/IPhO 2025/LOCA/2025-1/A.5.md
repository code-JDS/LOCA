# Refined Solution
### Problem Statement Explanation
This problem requires us to analyze the fine and hyperfine structures of the hydrogen atom based on the Bohr model. We are asked to perform two main tasks:

1.  **Fine Structure Energy Difference (\(\Delta E_F\)):** We need to derive an expression for the energy difference \(\Delta E_F\) between the two possible spin orientations of an electron in the ground state. This energy difference arises from the interaction of the electron's magnetic moment with the magnetic field created by the proton's apparent motion. The final expression for \(\Delta E_F\) must be a function of the fine-structure constant, \(\alpha\), and the ground state energy of the hydrogen atom, \(E_1\).

2.  **Hyperfine Transition Wavelength (\(\lambda_{HF}\)):** We need to find the wavelength, \(\lambda_{HF}\), of the photon emitted during a transition between the two hyperfine states. The energy difference for this transition, \(\Delta E_{HF}\), is given by an approximate relation involving \(\Delta E_F\). We must provide both a symbolic expression for \(\lambda_{HF}\) and its numerical value, rounded to two significant digits.

We will use the following given information and results from previous sub-problems:
-   The magnitude of the electron's magnetic moment is given as \(\mathscr{M}_{s}=\frac{e}{m_{e}} \hbar\).
-   The relation for the hyperfine energy difference is \(\Delta E_{HF} \simeq 3.72 \frac{m_{e}}{m_{p}} \Delta E_{F}\), where \(m_e\) and \(m_p\) are the electron and proton masses, respectively.
-   The magnetic field generated by the proton's apparent motion at the electron's location is \(B_1 = \frac{\mu_0 e \alpha c}{4\pi r_1^2}\).
-   The ground state energy is \(E_1 = -\frac{1}{2} m_e c^2 \alpha^2\). Note that this energy is negative.
-   The Bohr radius is \(r_1 = \frac{\hbar}{m_e c \alpha}\).
-   The fine-structure constant is \(\alpha = \frac{e^2}{4\pi\varepsilon_0 \hbar c}\).
-   The relation between electric and magnetic constants is \(\mu_0 \varepsilon_0 c^2 = 1\).

### Step 1: Express the fine structure energy difference \(\Delta E_F\)
The fine structure energy difference \(\Delta E_F\) is the energy difference between the states where the electron's magnetic moment is anti-parallel and parallel to the internal magnetic field \(\vec{B}_1\).

**Principles/Original Formulas/Assumptions**
The potential energy \(U\) of a magnetic dipole moment \(\vec{\mathscr{M}}\) in an external magnetic field \(\vec{B}\) is:
\[\boxed{U = -\vec{\mathscr{M}} \cdot \vec{B}}\]
The magnitude of the electron's magnetic moment is given as:
\[\boxed{\mathscr{M}_{s}=\frac{e}{m_{e}} \hbar}\]

**Derivation**
The energy of the state where the electron's magnetic moment \(\vec{\mathscr{M}}_s\) is parallel to the magnetic field \(\vec{B}_1\) is \(E_{\parallel} = -\mathscr{M}_s B_1\).
The energy of the state where \(\vec{\mathscr{M}}_s\) is anti-parallel to \(\vec{B}_1\) is \(E_{\nparallel} = +\mathscr{M}_s B_1\).
The energy difference \(\Delta E_F\) is defined as the energy of the higher state minus the energy of the lower state:
\[\begin{align}
\Delta E_F = E_{\nparallel} - E_{\parallel} = (\mathscr{M}_s B_1) - (-\mathscr{M}_s B_1) = 2 \mathscr{M}_s B_1
\label{eq:delta_E_F_def} \tag{1}
\end{align}\]
We substitute the given expression for \(\mathscr{M}_s\) and the expression for \(B_1\) from sub-problem A.4:
\[\begin{align}
\Delta E_F &= 2 \left(\frac{e\hbar}{m_e}\right) \left(\frac{\mu_0 e \alpha c}{4\pi r_1^2}\right) = \frac{2 \mu_0 e^2 \hbar \alpha c}{4\pi m_e r_1^2} \label{eq:delta_E_F_sub} \tag{2}
\end{align}\]
To express this in terms of \(\alpha\) and \(E_1\), we use the relations \(\mu_0 = 1/(\varepsilon_0 c^2)\) and \(\frac{e^2}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} = \alpha \hbar c\):
\[\begin{align}
\Delta E_F &= \frac{2}{\varepsilon_0 c^2} \frac{e^2 \hbar \alpha c}{4\pi m_e r_1^2} = 2 \left(\frac{e^2}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\right) \frac{\hbar \alpha}{m_e c r_1^2} = 2 (\alpha \hbar c) \frac{\hbar \alpha}{m_e c r_1^2} = \frac{2 \alpha^2 \hbar^2}{m_e r_1^2} \label{eq:delta_E_F_intermediate} \tag{3}
\end{align}\]
Now, we substitute the expression for the Bohr radius, \(r_1 = \frac{\hbar}{m_e c \alpha}\):
\[\begin{align}
\Delta E_F &= \frac{2 \alpha^2 \hbar^2}{m_e} \left(\frac{1}{r_1^2}\right) = \frac{2 \alpha^2 \hbar^2}{m_e} \left(\frac{m_e c \alpha}{\hbar}\right)^2 = \frac{2 \alpha^2 \hbar^2}{m_e} \frac{m_e^2 c^2 \alpha^2}{\hbar^2} = 2 m_e c^2 \alpha^4 \label{eq:delta_E_F_alpha} \tag{4}
\end{align}\]
From sub-problem A.3, the ground state energy is \(E_1 = -\frac{1}{2} m_e c^2 \alpha^2\). The magnitude of this energy is \(|E_1| = \frac{1}{2} m_e c^2 \alpha^2\), which implies \(m_e c^2 = \frac{2|E_1|}{\alpha^2}\). Substituting this into Eq. \eqref{eq:delta_E_F_alpha}:
\[\begin{align}
\Delta E_F = 2 \left(\frac{2|E_1|}{\alpha^2}\right) \alpha^4 = 4\alpha^2 |E_1| \label{eq:delta_E_F_E1_mag} \tag{5}
\end{align}\]
Since the problem asks for the expression in terms of \(E_1\) and we know \(E_1\) is negative (\(E_1 = -|E_1|\)), we can write:
\[\begin{align}
\Delta E_F = -4\alpha^2 E_1 \label{eq:delta_E_F_final} \tag{6}
\end{align}\]

### Step 2: Express the hyperfine transition wavelength \(\lambda_{HF}\)
The wavelength of the emitted photon is related to the energy difference of the hyperfine transition, \(\Delta E_{HF}\).

**Principles/Original Formulas/Assumptions**
The energy of a photon is related to its wavelength by:
\[\boxed{\Delta E = \frac{hc}{\lambda} = \frac{2\pi\hbar c}{\lambda}}\]
The hyperfine energy splitting is given by:
\[\boxed{\Delta E_{HF} \simeq 3.72 \frac{m_{e}}{m_{p}} \Delta E_{F}}\]

**Derivation**
From the energy-wavelength relation, we can express the wavelength \(\lambda_{HF}\) as:
\[\begin{align}
\lambda_{HF} = \frac{2\pi\hbar c}{\Delta E_{HF}} \label{eq:lambda_def} \tag{7}
\end{align}\]
Using the given relation for \(\Delta E_{HF}\) and our result for \(\Delta E_F\) from Eq. \eqref{eq:delta_E_F_final}:
\[\begin{align}
\lambda_{HF} &= \frac{2\pi\hbar c}{3.72 \frac{m_e}{m_p} \Delta E_F} = \frac{2\pi\hbar c}{3.72 \frac{m_e}{m_p} (-4\alpha^2 E_1)} \nonumber \\
&= -\frac{2\pi\hbar c}{4 \times 3.72 \frac{m_e}{m_p} \alpha^2 E_1} \nonumber \\
&= -\frac{\pi\hbar c}{2 \times 3.72 \frac{m_e}{m_p} \alpha^2 E_1} \label{eq:lambda_final_expr} \tag{8}
\end{align}\]
Note that since \(E_1\) is negative, the expression for \(\lambda_{HF}\) is positive, as expected for a physical wavelength.

### Step 3: Numerical Calculation of \(\lambda_{HF}\)
We now compute the numerical value using the derived expressions and given constants.

**Derivation**
First, we calculate the value of \(\Delta E_F\) using the positive expression from Eq. \eqref{eq:delta_E_F_E1_mag} and the values \(\alpha \approx 7.27 \times 10^{-3}\) and \(|E_1| \approx 13.5 \text{ eV}\).
\[\begin{align}
\Delta E_F = 4\alpha^2 |E_1| = 4 \times (7.27 \times 10^{-3})^2 \times 13.5 \text{ eV} \approx 2.854 \times 10^{-3} \text{ eV} \label{eq:delta_E_F_num} \tag{9}
\end{align}\]
Next, we calculate \(\Delta E_{HF}\). The mass ratio is \(\frac{m_e}{m_p} \approx \frac{9.109 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}}{1.672 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}} \approx 5.448 \times 10^{-4}\).
\[\begin{align}
\Delta E_{HF} &\approx 3.72 \times (5.448 \times 10^{-4}) \times (2.854 \times 10^{-3} \text{ eV}) \nonumber \\
&\approx 5.778 \times 10^{-6} \text{ eV} \label{eq:delta_E_HF_num} \tag{10}
\end{align}\]
Finally, we calculate the wavelength \(\lambda_{HF}\) using the value \(hc \approx 1240 \text{ eV}\cdot\text{nm}\).
\[\begin{align}
\lambda_{HF} = \frac{hc}{\Delta E_{HF}} &\approx \frac{1240 \text{ eV}\cdot\text{nm}}{5.778 \times 10^{-6} \text{ eV}} \nonumber \\
&\approx 2.146 \times 10^8 \text{ nm} \nonumber \\
&\approx 0.2146 \text{ m} \approx 21.46 \text{ cm} \label{eq:lambda_num} \tag{11}
\end{align}\]
Rounding to two significant digits as requested:
\[\begin{align}
\lambda_{HF} \approx 21 \text{ cm} \label{eq:lambda_rounded} \tag{12}
\end{align}\]

### Final Answer
The fine structure energy difference is expressed as a function of \(\alpha\) and \(E_1\) as:
\[\begin{align}
\Delta E_F = -4\alpha^2 E_1
\end{align}\]
The wavelength of a photon emitted during a transition between the two states of the hyperfine structure is given by the expression:
\[\begin{align}
\lambda_{HF} = -\frac{\pi\hbar c}{2 \times 3.72 \frac{m_e}{m_p} \alpha^2 E_1}
\end{align}\]
Its numerical value is:
\[\begin{align}
\boxed{\lambda_{HF} \approx 21 \text{ cm}}
\end{align}\]
